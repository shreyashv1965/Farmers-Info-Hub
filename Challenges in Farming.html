1. Climate Change

Unpredictable weather patterns, droughts, and floods disrupt crop cycles. Rising temperatures affect crop yield and livestock health.

2. Soil Degradation

Excessive use of chemical fertilizers and monoculture practices reduce soil fertility. Soil erosion and salinization further decrease arable land.

3. Water Scarcity

Limited freshwater availability hampers irrigation and crop production. Overextraction of groundwater leads to long-term shortages.

4. Pests and Diseases

Emergence of resistant pests and pathogens poses a significant threat. Reduced biodiversity in some farming systems increases susceptibility.

5. Economic Pressures

High costs of seeds, fertilizers, and machinery strain small and marginal farmers. Fluctuations in market prices create uncertainty for producers.

6. Technology Accessibility

Small-scale farmers may lack access to modern tools and innovations. Lack of training and awareness of sustainable practices impacts productivity.

7. Labor Shortages

Migration to urban areas reduces the workforce available for farming activities. Dependency on manual labor makes farming operations challenging.

8. Policy and Support

Limited government support for small-scale farmers in some regions. Difficulty in accessing credit and subsidies affects their growth.
